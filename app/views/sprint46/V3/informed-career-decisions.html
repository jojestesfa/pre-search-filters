Make informed career decisions

When you make a career decision you may want to think about:

  • the sort of person you are
  • what work you're a good fit for
  • what you’re good at and what skills you have

It's also useful to consider:

  • what jobs exist, in what sectors and who does them
  • which regions have the most jobs in a specific sector
  • which jobs are growing in demand, and which are declining
  • what skills and qualifications employers need
  • whether the job you do is likely to exist in the future
  • how much people earn and how long they work for
  • where and how people work

Career decisions in the real world

Labour market information (LMI) is everything we can measure about the world of work. LMI can help you make realistic and informed career decisions.

LMI comes from lots of different sources. These include:

  • government and industry surveys
  • reports from professional bodies
  • reports that focus on specific groups of people. These may be graduates, women, older workers or people with disabilities

The facts and figures can sometimes be difficult to understand. Careers advisers interpret and simplify complex labour market information. Advisers help you make career decisions based on your individual circumstances and goals.

Labour market information in real life

Labour market information can help when you’re thinking about questions like:

  • how easy is this job to get into?
  • if I take this course will I get a job at the end?
  • how much will I get paid when I’ve done this job for a few years?
  • will I need to move area to do this career?
  • will this job still be around in 10 years’ time?

It's important to have accurate and up to date information about the world of work. This will help you to make realistic and informed choices.

It can help you to see what you’ll need to be able to start or get on in your chosen career, whether it's:

  • skills
  • qualifications
  • experience

This will help you make a plan and give you a clear sense of direction.

Labour market information on our site

To help you in your career journey, labour market information makes up a part of everything we do. You might find it useful to:

  • find out salaries, working hours and patterns for a range of jobs in explore careers
  • compare salary, working hours and employment outlook between jobs using the Careerometer
