Understand the world of work

Labour market information (LMI) tells us about the world of work. It can tell you a lot about a range of careers including:

  • different ways of getting into a career
  • how many job opportunities there are for specific jobs
  • how many job opportunities there are likely to be in the future
  • how easy it is to get promoted in a specific job role
  • how much you can earn in a job
  • what skills and qualifications you need to do a job

It can also be useful in making decisions about your career.

How it can help you as a career starter

As a career starter, you've got a lot to think about.

Careers advisers often help students with questions about what subjects to choose. If you know the job areas that are going to grow, you can choose the right subjects at GCSE, college and university. It can also help you identify what skills you'll need to develop.

There are also decisions about what to do at 16 and what to do at 18 to think about. You can also use LMI to find out about:

  • different routes into jobs
  • changes to available entry routes

At the start of your career, you have an opportunity to identify trends in the world of work like:

  • which job sectors are growing and which are declining
  • what types of work are available near you
  • whether you'll need to move for your career

This information can help you choose a job with good longer-term prospects.

How it can help you as a career changer

There can be a lot to think about when you're considering moving career. One of the primary concerns of people changing career is whether you’re likely to find a job after investing in training.

LMI can help you work out what the risks are so you can feel confident in your decision. It can help you identify:

  • the ages of people in entry-level roles in the jobs you're looking at
  • the employability rates in the sector or occupation you’re interested in
  • related jobs which use skills you already have
  • skills gaps you could train to fill
  • whether there are jobs in your new occupation in your local area

This information can help you identify different ways of changing career, and make sure that you’re able to make a decision that works for you and your circumstances.

How it can help me progress in my career

LMI can also be helpful when you’re looking at different ways to progress in your career. It is especially helpful when it comes to training and professional development in your workplace. It can help you to work out if your job or job sector is in decline so that you can take steps to protect yourself. You could:

  • see if there are skills shortages in related disciplines that you’d be interested in looking into
  • identify opportunities to upskill or learn a discipline that complements your existing role
  • discover jobs you could transfer to with your existing skillset, either within your organisation or with another organisation

The world of work is always changing so keeping up to date with labour market information can help you to identify trends and prepare for them.

How can it help someone facing redundancy

Redundancy can come as a shock at the best of times, but the more you know, the better prepared you will be for taking your next steps. Using LMI, you can find out if you’ve been redundant because your job isn’t needed in the organisation any more, or if there’s a bigger trend that applies to your role in all business. Once you know this, you can:

  • see if there are many jobs in your chosen role in your local area or if you need to move to continue working
  • identify related jobs you’d be able to do
  • retrain in a new skill, discipline or occupation
  • identify routes into a new career that suit your circumstances and financial needs

If you decide to retrain after being made redundant, it can feel intimidating to return to the world of work in an entry-level role. LMI can help you find out more about age trends within a given occupation or sector so you can identify sectors where age and professional experience is an asset, like:

  • counselling and coaching
  • teaching
  • training and instruction
  • social work

Take your next steps in the world of work

Now that you've found out about the world of work, you might want to:

  • take a skills assessment and find out what jobs might suit you
  • explore careers that interest you
  • look for careers advice to help you look for and apply for jobs

You can also speak to an adviser about your next steps by phone, email or webchat if you want more support with your career decisions.
