Understand the world of work

Understand the world of work and get to know how labour market information helps you make realistic and informed career decisions.

Labour market information explained

Labour market information (LMI) is everything we can measure and analyse about the world of work. It covers information that can tell us things like:

  • what jobs exist and in what sectors
  • which regions have the most jobs in a specific sector
  • which jobs are growing in demand or likely to disappear in the future
  • what skills and qualifications employers need
  • facts about equality and diversity at work
  • how much people earn, their hours, where and how they work

Labour market information comes from lots of different sources like government and industry surveys, reports from professional bodies or organisations that deal with specific groups of people for example, graduates, women or people with a disability. The facts and figures can sometimes be hard to understand. Careers advice professionals spend a lot of time interpreting labour market information in a way that can be meaningful and useful to you.

How labour market information can help

Labour market information can help when you’re thinking about questions like:

  • how easy is this job to get into?
  • if I take this course will I get a job at the end?
  • how much will I get paid when I’ve been doing this job for a few years?
  • will I need to move area to do this career?
  • will this job still be around in 10 years’ time?

Accurate and up to date information on the world of work can help you to decide on the right career path for you and your circumstances. It can help you to see what skills, qualifications or experience you’ll need to be able to start or get on in your chosen career. This will help you make a plan and give you a clear sense of direction.

Make sure you have the real facts

Good career decisions are based on reliable information. When you’re looking at labour market information, there are some important things to check. Make sure that the information:

  • comes from a reliable and trusted source
  • is up to date
  • gives an accurate picture of what is really happening (looking at 2 or 3 different pieces of information on the same thing can help)
